The fastest ways to learn and actually remember what you've learned. 1. Feynman's Technique: 1) Study a topic, 2) Expain it to a 5-year-old, 3) Fill in the knowledge gaps, 4) Simplify and repeat.
2. Handwriting > Typing: Use your old pen and paper to capture notes. Your memory will thank you for it.
3. Spaced Repetition: Review your notes regularly to retain new concepts. The spacing effect allows for your learnings to solidify overtime.
4. The Pareto Rule: 80% of results come from 20% of efforts. Spend time thinking about your learning roadmap, like figuring out the top 10 key skills for your career goals and mastering those.
5. Vary your Sources: Read, view, listen, discuss ... Stimulating your brain in different ways engages more of it. Chances are, you'll gain more perspectives, so you'll remember things for longer.
