Column-1 Completing the Whole Course First and Doing Practical at the End: Advantages: Provides a comprehensive understanding of the theoretical aspects before diving into practical applications. Allows for a structured learning progression where foundational knowledge is built upon. May be suitable for subjects where theoretical understanding is crucial before attempting practical tasks. Disadvantages: Risk of forgetting theoretical concepts if there's a long gap between learning and applying them practically. Practical applications may seem disconnected from the theoretical concepts if not applied immediately. Doing Practical Alongside the Course: Advantages: Immediate application of theoretical concepts reinforces learning and improves retention. Helps in understanding the relevance of theoretical concepts in real-world scenarios. Encourages active engagement and problem-solving skills. Disadvantages: May feel overwhelming for some learners, especially if practical tasks are complex and require a strong theoretical foundation. Requires careful planning to ensure that practical activities align with the course content without overwhelming learners.
